Vanishing Wonders: The Urgent Call to Save Our Species

INTRODUCTION

Extinction is the process through which a species, genus, or family, becomes permanently lost. It means that no individuals of the species are left alive. Endangered species are species that are at significant risk of extinction in the near future.

Historical Perspective: - Historically, extinction events have occurred naturally, such as the asteroid impact that led to the extinction of the dinosaurs around 65 million years ago. - The current rate of extinction is much higher than natural background rates, largely due to human activities.

Importance of Biodiversity: - Biodiversity refers to the variety of life on Earth, including different species, ecosystems, and genetic diversity within species. - High biodiversity ensures ecosystem productivity, resilience, and provides resources for humans, such as medicine, food, and clean water.

SPECIES ON THE BRINK

Amur Leopard

Habitat: Temperate forests of the Russian Far East and China.
Population: Fewer than 100 individuals.
Threats: Habitat loss, poaching, prey depletion.

Javan Rhino

Habitat: Tropical forests of Ujung Kulon National Park, Indonesia. Population: Around 60 individuals. Threats: Habitat destruction, natural disasters, disease.

Vaquita

Habitat: Northern part of the Gulf of California, Mexico. Population: Fewer than 10 individuals. Threats: Bycatch in illegal gillnets, habitat degradation.
